If you've been editing a single `index.html` and dragging it to Netlify, you already know the pain: no real URLs, no reusable components, every change means an FTP upload (or a drag-and-drop), and forget about a blog or a contact form.

Next.js App Router fixes all of this. Every file in `app/` is a route. `app/services/page.tsx` is `yoursite.com/services`. There's nothing to configure.

## What you get for free

- **Real URLs** — `/services`, `/blog/post-slug`, whatever you want.
- **Server components** — fast initial loads, less client JS.
- **File-based routing** — your filesystem is your site map.
- **MDX support** — write content in markdown, drop in React components when you need them.

## The migration story

1. Copy the bits of your existing HTML into `app/page.tsx`.
2. Move your "other pages" into `app/<page-name>/page.tsx`.
3. Replace `<a href="...">` with `<Link href="...">` from `next/link`.
4. Deploy to Vercel. Push to deploy forever after.

That's it. You're on a modern stack.
